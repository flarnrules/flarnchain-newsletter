Market Update / Weekly Chart

This week, the crypto markets were pretty weird. They were down pretty bad at the beginning, off the heels of a 10%+ drop in the market price of just about everything in crypto. Pretty "crypto-y" behavior for the market, but still a 10% change in the price of an asset will never not be jarring. All that said, this week the crypto market shot up rapidly (actually this happened yesterday) due to some good news for a large player in the crypto industry Grayscale. So anyways, it's been a mixed bag. It's also important to note that the "big increase" looks a lot less extreme when viewed at a distance:

Sure, the market is up 5-10% this week, but look at how that looks on the chart? Actually, now that I'm really looking at this weekly chart, I think there are a few ways I could improve it for better readability. First, I think this chart would work better as 2 charts. I'm trying to cram way too much information on one screen. I'm going to work on this next week and come up with a better way to look at the % change chart and the Bitcoin chart simultaneously, but in a way that is hopefully less visually noisy.

If we look at the current cumulative change for this year, we can see that Bitcoin is still up almost 20%... which is quite a lot when considering that the typical returns that investors are seeking are in that sweet spot of 6-8% every single year. a 20% year is like 3 years worth of traditional finance gains. Now you would have needed to buy Bitcoin at exactly the right date, and then sell all of your Bitcoin... I guess today to make that type of gain, but still. It's crazy to think that 20% up on the year is considered poor returns. Then again, holding an asset like Bitcoin carries massive risk, so in that context it absolutely makes sense. High risk - high reward baby!

The start of my journey to self-hosting this newsletter

The more I try to figure out how to automate the process of sending this newsletter to a larger audience, the more I realize that I'm either stuck paying for a service, or building something a lot more complex than I am capable of doing with my current set of skills. I basically have three choices:

    Learn something new
    Pay for a service
    Do both!

In case you haven't guessed it yet, I've decided on the third option "do both". In that spirit, I'm researching basically everything there is to research regarding email. We are talking stuff like SMTP (simple mail transfer protocol), DNS (domain name system), SPF (sender policy framework), DKIM (domainkeys identified mail standard), DMARC (domain-based message authentication, reporting, and conformance) and whole bunch of other stuff.

Why am I doing this? Because I want to understand the underlying tools that we use every day to transfer information. If I don't understand that basic infrastructure, how the heck am I to truly understand the future of financial infrastructure? The backbone of all of this stuff is the Internet, so if I don't really understand how stuff on the internet works, then I probably shouldn't be playing around with financial rails. Haha, just kidding. You don't need to know how the engine of a car works to drive a car... then again. With Flarnchain, I'm not trying to use a blockchain... I'm trying to build one.
