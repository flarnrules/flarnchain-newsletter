The Bitcoin Protocol

Let's take a moment to revisit a concept I breifly touched on near the beginning of this newsletter. The Bitcoin protocol. I don't think it's a good idea to get deep in the weeds of the Bitcoin protocol, that can be reserved for future newsletters. Let's just keep things simple. The Bitcoin protocol is the set of rules that governs how Bitcoin works. These rules are hard to change dramatically. The protocol is tweaked occasionally with software upgrades called "hard forks" and "soft forks" but the major components of the protocol remain unchanged Bitcoin's genesis block way back in 2009. The process by which Bitcoin is chainged is a fascinating topic, but oustide the scope of tonight's newsletter.

This is a Bitcoin. It is "saying" a Bitcoin wallet address.

There are basically two "pillars" in the Bitcoin protocol that are important to get started thinking about Bitcoin.

    1 A capped supply of 21 million Bitcoins meaning that only 21 million Bitcoins will ever be "mined" by Bitcoin miners
    2 A halving event that occurs once every roughly 4 years meaning that every 4 years, 50% fewer Bitcoins are "mined" each time a new Bitcoin block is created, which each block is created roughly once every 10 minutes

In this image, Bitcoin is indicating that it has not reached it's final form.

Pillar #2 above is super important, as it has dictated the way the crypto markets have ebbed and flowed since the inception of Bitcoin in 2009. If we think about a Bitcoin halving event in simple terms of supply and demand... this makes perfect sense. All things held equal, if the new supply of Bitcoin every 10 minutes is cut in half, but demand for Bitcoin remains the same, then the price of Bitcoin will increase. Now, many people are aware of this concept, and thus plan accordingly and act to front run this essential element of the Bitcoin protocol. But, not everyone does this sort of front running, because not everyone is even aware of the halving event. Each cycle (that is the 4 year Bitcoin halving cycle) the impact that this event has on the price of Bitcoin diminishes. This will be the 4th such cycle, and my prediction is that it will still have a noticable increase in the price of Bitcoin resulting in anywhere from a 100% increase to a 200% increase in the price of Bitcoin in the days leading up to the halving.

If the macro economic conditions are ripe for a sort of "risk on" investing environment, this could result in a cascading effect of first institutional buyers, and then retail buyers piling on and trying to buy Bitcoin when the supply of newly minted Bitcoins has been recently halved. Miners will want to hold some of their Bitcoins if the price is rising, resulting in even more supply strain, and creating a virtious feedback loop causing massive price action to the upside. This has happened several times in the past, and there's no indication that it won't happen again. The new capital that floods into the markets will propel other, much smaller crypto assets up by much larger multiples, which is where both the largest amount of risk resides, and the largest amount of upside potential.If true, my prediction is that this will then kick off a period of intense speculation and another rip-roaring crypto bull market.

If the halving even kicks off another bull market, and another cambrian explosion of innovation and wealth generation in the crypto ecosystem, I intend to capitalize heavily on this event, but I also have learned lessons from the past. The vast majority of the potential gains in the next bull market will not be sustainable and holding an asset that has gone up by 100% or in some cases 1,000% in a short period of time is a recipe for disaster. Thus, I'm currently formulating a plan that will methodically enter into, and exit out of, winning positions if this bull market materializes.
